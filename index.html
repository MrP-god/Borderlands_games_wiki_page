Introduction

Welcome to the comprehensive wiki for the Borderlands series, the popular looter-shooter franchise developed by Gearbox Software known for its distinctive cel-shaded art style, irreverent humor, and billions of procedurally generated weapons.

Borderlands (2009)

Borderlands was the first entry in the franchise, introducing players to the wasteland planet of Pandora. This game established the core gameplay loop of questing, looting, and shooting that would define the series.

The game follows four Vault Hunters - Roland, Lilith, Mordecai, and Brick - as they search for an ancient alien vault rumored to contain advanced technology and untold riches.

Key Features

  • Four playable characters with unique skill trees
  • Procedurally generated weapons system with millions of possible combinations
  • RPG elements including experience points, skill trees, and character building
  • Cooperative multiplayer for up to 4 players
## Rating - Metacritic Score: 81/100

Borderlands 2 (2012)

Widely considered the pinnacle of the series, Borderlands 2 expanded on everything that made the original great while introducing one of gaming's most memorable villains: Handsome Jack.

Set five years after the events of the original, Borderlands 2 follows a new group of Vault Hunters - Axton, Maya, Salvador, Zero, Gaige, and Krieg - as they join the resistance against Handsome Jack and the Hyperion Corporation.

Key Features

  • Six playable characters (including two DLC characters)
  • Greatly expanded narrative with more developed characters
  • Enhanced co-op mechanics and gameplay refinements
  • More diverse environments beyond the wastelands of the original
## Rating - Metacritic Score: 89/100

Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel (2014)

Set between the events of Borderlands and Borderlands 2, The Pre-Sequel explores the rise of Handsome Jack from company programmer to megalomaniacal villain. The game takes place on Pandora's moon, Elpis, introducing low-gravity gameplay and new oxygen mechanics.

Players take on the roles of Wilhelm, Athena, Nisha, Claptrap, Aurelia, and Jack's body double as they witness Jack's transformation firsthand.

Key Features

  • Low-gravity environment with unique movement mechanics
  • New "Oz kit" equipment slot for oxygen and special abilities
  • Cryo and laser weapon types
  • "Butt slam" ground pound attack utilizing the low gravity
## Rating - Metacritic Score: 75/100

Tales from the Borderlands (2014-2015)

A departure from the main series' first-person shooter gameplay, Tales from the Borderlands is an episodic narrative adventure game developed by Telltale Games. The story follows Rhys, a Hyperion employee, and Fiona, a con artist, as their paths intertwine in search of a Vault.

Key Features

  • Episodic narrative-driven gameplay
  • Choice-based dialogue and actions that affect the story
  • Characters and events that connect to the main series
  • Trademark Borderlands humor with a stronger focus on storytelling
## Rating - Metacritic Score: 86/100

Borderlands 3 (2019)

Borderlands 3 expanded the universe beyond Pandora, taking players to various planets as they battle the Calypso Twins, streamers who have united the bandit clans and seek to open Vaults across the galaxy.

Players choose between four new Vault Hunters: Amara, FL4K, Moze, and Zane, each with their own unique abilities and skill trees.

Key Features

  • Multiple explorable planets beyond Pandora
  • Enhanced movement mechanics including sliding and mantling
  • Alternative firing modes for weapons
  • Expanded skill trees and action skills customization
## Rating - Metacritic Score: 78/100

Tiny Tina's Wonderlands (2022)

A spin-off inspired by the popular Borderlands 2 DLC "Tiny Tina's Assault on Dragon Keep," this game takes place within an in-universe tabletop role-playing game called "Bunkers & Badasses," with Tiny Tina as the dungeon master.

The game combines Borderlands' shooting mechanics with fantasy elements like spellcasting and melee weapons.

Key Features

  • Fantasy setting with guns, magic, and melee combat
  • Character creator with customizable class combinations
  • Overworld map for traveling between main areas
  • Multi-class system allowing for diverse character builds
## Rating - Metacritic Score: 75/100

New Tales from the Borderlands (2022)

A standalone sequel to Tales from the Borderlands, this narrative adventure follows three new protagonists - Anu, Octavio, and Fran - as they face a planetary invasion and corrupt corporate overlords on the planet Promethea.

Key Features

  • Choice-based narrative gameplay
  • New characters with connections to the broader Borderlands universe
  • Quick-time events and minigames integrated into the story
  • Multiple endings based on player choices
## Rating - Metacritic Score: 65/100
